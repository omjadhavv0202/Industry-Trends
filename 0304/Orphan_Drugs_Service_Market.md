 Orphan drug services refer to providing professional technical support to pharmaceutical companies to help them develop, produce and market orphan drugs. Orphan drugs, also known as rare drugs, are drugs used to prevent, treat, and diagnose rare diseases. Due to the small number of people suffering from rare diseases, low market demand, and high research and development costs, few pharmaceutical companies pay attention to the research and development of their therapeutic drugs. Therefore, these Drugs are vividly called "orphan drugs". The main contents of orphan drug services include drug target screening, pharmacodynamic research, pharmaceutical research, pharmacokinetic research, clinical trial plan design and implementation, etc. These services are designed to ensure that orphan drugs meet clinical needs and regulatory requirements in terms of drug mechanism of action, strength, dosage, safety and effectiveness.</p><p>
