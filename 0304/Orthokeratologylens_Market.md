 </p><p><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>3. What is orthokeratology, and how do orthokeratology lenses work?</strong></span></p><p>
 
 </p><p>Orthokeratology, or Ortho-K, refers to a non-surgical treatment where special contact lenses are worn overnight to reshape the cornea and correct refractive errors like myopia (nearsightedness). The lenses are typically worn while sleeping and removed during the day, allowing for clear vision without the need for daytime glasses or contacts.</p><p>
 
 </p><p><span style="font-size:16px;"><strong>4. What are the benefits of using orthokeratology lenses?</strong></span></p><p>
 
 </p><p>The main benefits of orthokeratology lenses include improved vision correction without the need for daytime glasses or contact lenses, reduced risk of eye strain, and the potential to slow the progression of myopia in children. It is a non-surgical alternative to LASIK for vision correction.</p><p>
