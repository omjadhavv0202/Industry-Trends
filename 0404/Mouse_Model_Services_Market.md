These models are non-human animals used to mimic human diseases, providing invaluable insights without posing risks to human life. Mouse models are indispensable in preclinical research, helping scientists explore genetic, pharmacological, and therapeutic interventions for a variety of diseases such as cancer, neurological disorders, cardiovascular conditions, and infectious diseases.</p>
 
 
 
 <p>? </p>
 
 
 
 <p>These models offer significant advantages in medical studies, allowing researchers to study complex biological processes, evaluate the effects of drugs, and assess therapeutic interventions. They are widely utilized in academic and research institutes, as well as pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies. With advancements in genetic engineering, the <strong>scope</strong> of mouse model services has expanded, enabling the creation of more specialized models, such as genetically modified or immunodeficient mice, to explore specific disease mechanisms and treatment responses.</p>
