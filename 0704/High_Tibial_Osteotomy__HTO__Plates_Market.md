 <p>High tibial osteotomy (HTO) is a simpler surgery that aims to realign the knee joint of there is wear on one side more than the other. It involves inserting a wedge into the tibia (shin bone) near the knee joint in order to move the force involved when a person is walking onto the healthy side of their knee joint. This surgery can delay the point where a knee replacement is necessary by 8-10 years.</p>
