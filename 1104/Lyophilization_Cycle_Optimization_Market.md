Lyophilization is a dehydration process that involves freezing the material to be dried, followed by the removal of ice or frozen solvent by sublimation under vacuum conditions. This technique is widely used in the pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and food industries to preserve heat-sensitive or unstable materials in a stable, dry form.</p>
