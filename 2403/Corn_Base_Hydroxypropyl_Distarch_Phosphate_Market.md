 <p data-end="571" data-start="109"><span style="font-size:16px;">Corn Base Hydroxypropyl Distarch Phosphate (HPDS) is a modified starch product derived from corn. It is chemically altered through a process that introduces hydroxypropyl groups into the starch molecules, improving the starch&#39;s functional properties, such as its stability, emulsifying capacity, and resistance to heat and acids. As a result, it serves as an excellent emulsifier, stabilizer, and thickener in various industries, particularly in the food sector.</span></p>
 
 
 
 <p>? </p>
 
 
 
 <p data-end="993" data-start="573"><span style="font-size:16px;">In food production, Corn Base Hydroxypropyl Distarch Phosphate is commonly used as a food binder, emulsifier, and stabilizer. Additionally, it serves as a fiber supplement, providing dietary fiber to food products, which contributes to digestive health.</span></p>
