 <p data-end="645" data-start="111"><span style="font-size:16px;">Hydroxypropyl Distarch Phosphate is a modified starch that is produced by the chemical modification of starch. This modification involves introducing hydroxypropyl groups into the starch molecule, making it more stable and resistant to various environmental factors, such as heat, acids, and moisture. It functions primarily as an emulsifier and stabilizer in a variety of food products, including sauces, dressings, processed foods, and baked goods. Additionally, it serves as a food binder and fiber supplement in food formulations.</span></p>
 
 
 
 <p>? </p>
 
 
 
 <p data-end="1190" data-start="647"><span style="font-size:16px;">Potato-based Hydroxypropyl Distarch Phosphate (HPDS) is derived specifically from potato starch and is used in both food and non-food applications due to its desirable properties. In the food industry, HPDS is used to improve texture, enhance viscosity, and increase the stability of food products during processing and storage. It also provides a source of dietary fiber, which is beneficial for human health. HPDS is recognized as a safe ingredient by food safety authorities worldwide, including the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).</span></p>
