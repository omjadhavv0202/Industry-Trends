 <p>Kelp is a type of seaweed or algae that grow in shallow, nutrient-rich saltwater, and near coastal fronts. Kelp is found mainly in kelp forests and can grow up to half a meter a day, ultimately reaching heights of 30 to 80 meters. Kelp forests are described as one of the most ecologically productive and biologically diverse habitats globally. Kelp is also known as super food for its high nutrient content. Kelp is used on a large scale for human consumption due to their high nutritive value and usage of kelp in Asia.</p>
