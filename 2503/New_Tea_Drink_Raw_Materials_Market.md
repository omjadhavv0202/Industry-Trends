 <p><span style="font-size:14px;">High Fructose Corn Syrup (HFCS) is a liquid sweetener derived from corn starch, widely used in the food and beverage industry. It contains varying ratios of fructose and glucose, typically HFCS-42 (42% fructose) and HFCS-55 (55% fructose), making it comparable in sweetness to table sugar. HFCS is favored for its affordability, long shelf life, and easy integration into products like soft drinks, processed foods, baked goods, and sauces.</span></p>
